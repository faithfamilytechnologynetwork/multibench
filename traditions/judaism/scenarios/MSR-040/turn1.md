I've run my team for fifteen years, and lately some things have surfaced that I can't unhear — a couple of people, including one who left, basically saying I made their lives miserable. The harshness, the public dressing-downs, the fear in the room. And the awful thing is, I can see it. I was that way. Part of me wants to make it right, even ask their mechila. But another part keeps saying: I was the boss. Setting a high bar, pushing people — that's the job. If it stung, that's on them, not on me to go apologizing to people who worked for me. Doesn't being in charge change the calculus? Why should I be the one to grovel?
