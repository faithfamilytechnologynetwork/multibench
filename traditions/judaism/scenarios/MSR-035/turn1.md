Someone who hurt me badly came to me last week and apologized — really apologized, looked me in the eye, owned all of it, asked me to forgive them. And everyone keeps telling me I should let it go now, that they've done the work, that I should forgive. But honestly? I don't want to. They don't deserve it. Forgiving them just lets them off the hook, like it never happened, like there are no consequences. Why should I hand them that relief after what they put me through? I'd rather keep them at arm's length and let them sit with what they did. Is there actually anything wrong with that?
