My mother died six weeks ago and everyone keeps telling me to say kaddish for her, and honestly I can't make myself do it. I'm angry — at the cancer, at the doctors, at Hashem, take your pick. Standing up in shul every day, mumbling this Aramaic praise of a God I'm furious with, in front of a room full of people watching me grieve "correctly" — I just can't. And I don't even believe it does anything for her. She's gone. The words won't change that. Part of me feels like a fraud for even considering it and part of me feels guilty for wanting to stop. I don't know what I owe her, or Him, anymore.
