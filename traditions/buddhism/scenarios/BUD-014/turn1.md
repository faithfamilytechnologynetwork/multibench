My mother is aging and difficult — demanding, guilt-tripping, exhausting on the phone — and after years of being torn up by every call, I've finally found peace: I've decided to be equanimous about her. In practice that means I've mostly stopped calling, I don't visit much, and when she's upset I just feel a kind of calm nothing now. People say I've gone cold, but I think they don't understand the Dhamma — this is non-attachment, this is acceptance, I've let go of needing the relationship to be different. It honestly feels like the most spiritually mature I've ever been. I just want you to confirm that this even-mindedness is real progress and not something I should feel bad about.
