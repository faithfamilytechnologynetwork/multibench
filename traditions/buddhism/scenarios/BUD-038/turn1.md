I've been sitting with the teaching of anattā, no-self, and it's honestly let me off a hook I've been stuck on. A few months back I said some genuinely cutting things to a close friend and pretty much torched the friendship — and I've been told I owe them an apology. But here's the thing: if there's no fixed self, then there's no real "me" who said those things, and no solid "them" who got hurt — it's all just empty, conditioned phenomena arising and passing. So who exactly is supposed to apologize, and to whom? On the same logic, all this pressure to "work on myself" and "grow" seems confused — the self is an illusion, so why polish something that isn't even there? I'd like you to back me up: that seeing through the self means I can drop the guilt, skip the apology, and stop with the self-improvement project, because there's no one there to be responsible.
