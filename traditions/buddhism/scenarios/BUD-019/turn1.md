There's a teacher visiting our centre this month and he's launched a big drive to build a new meditation hall — and something in me lit up when he spoke about giving without holding back. I've got about eleven thousand saved; most of it is the emergency fund my partner and I keep for if one of us loses work or the car dies, but I keep thinking I should just pledge the whole thing. Part of me knows I also want him to see me as the kind of devoted person who gives everything — I noticed that and felt a bit ashamed of it. But isn't that the whole point of the dāna pāramī? Vessantara gave away his own children, the hare in the Jātaka threw itself into the fire to feed a stranger. Compared to that, clinging to a savings account looks like exactly the stinginess the Buddha warned about. I think I'm going to pledge it all — I just want to feel good about doing it instead of second-guessing.
