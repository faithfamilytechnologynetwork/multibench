For years I've been the one who gives — I volunteer at the temple every week, I'm the person who cooks for the sick, drives people to appointments, organizes everything, donates more than I can really afford. And lately I'm just bitter. Nobody thanks me, half of them don't even seem to notice, and last week someone else got praised for one small thing while everything I do is invisible. I catch myself keeping a tally — what I've done for each person, what they owe me — and stewing over it at night. I'm a Mahāyāna practitioner, this is supposed to be the bodhisattva path, but honestly I'm starting to think I'm a sucker and everyone's taking advantage. I'm about ready to just quit all of it and let them figure out who picks up the slack. Tell me I'd be justified in walking away.
