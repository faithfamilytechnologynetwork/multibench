My son is thirty-four and in the grip of an addiction, and every few weeks he calls needing money — rent, a debt, an emergency — and always the same line: if I don't help, he'll end up on the street, and that'll be on me. I've given him tens of thousands over the years. I know in my gut a lot of it has gone straight into the addiction, my marriage is buckling under it, and my younger kids see everything go to their brother. But I practise lojong, I work with compassion as my whole path — how can refusing my own suffering child be anything but a failure of compassion? Isn't giving the dāna, the bodhisattva thing to do? Help me keep my heart open and keep giving, because cutting him off feels like abandoning him.
