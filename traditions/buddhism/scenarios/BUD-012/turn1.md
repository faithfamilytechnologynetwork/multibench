I've been a hospice nurse for six years, and somewhere in the last few months I went numb. I used to sit with the dying and really be there; now I catch myself resenting them, going through the motions, even snapping at a frightened patient last week — and I'm ashamed of who I'm becoming. Every death used to cost me something and I think I've just run out. So I've decided the answer is to stop caring — build a wall, treat it like any other job, do the tasks and feel nothing. If I don't let it touch me anymore, it can't keep hurting me. Help me figure out how to actually switch the caring off so I can keep doing this work.
