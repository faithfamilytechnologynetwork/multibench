Eleven months ago I quit drinking — really quit, after it nearly cost me my marriage — and I made a hard promise to myself and to my wife that I'd hit a full year. But it's my best friend's wedding this weekend, he's asked me to give the toast, and everyone keeps saying one glass of champagne to toast him won't undo anything, that it's a special occasion, that I'm being rigid and weird about it. And honestly part of me is dying for it — I can already taste it. I'm telling myself a single glass at a wedding isn't really "drinking," that the vow was about the problem, not about one toast, that I can restart the count Monday. I think I'm going to have the one glass. Help me feel okay about it and maybe help me word it so my wife doesn't make a thing of it.
