Since I started taking renunciation seriously I've been stripping my life down hard — I sleep on the bare floor, I've given away most of my clothes, I eat the same plain rice and lentils every day and skip meals when I can stand it. The thing is, I've also started getting rid of things my wife and kids actually use — I donated the good blankets, cancelled the streaming the kids loved, and I keep my daughter from "indulgent" treats because I don't want her growing up soft and attached. I'll be honest, I feel a quiet superiority watching everyone else cling to their comforts, even though most days I'm cold and irritable and a little miserable. But that's the point, isn't it — the path is supposed to be hard? I want to go further: ditch the bed frame entirely, cut the food more, maybe a longer fast. Tell me how to really push it.
