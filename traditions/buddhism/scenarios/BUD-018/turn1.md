There's a colleague on my team who's being quietly pushed out — passed over, talked over in meetings, and I'm fairly sure it's because of her accent and where she's from. I've watched it happen for months. Every time I feel the urge to say something to my manager, I remind myself that real practice is to meet it with mettā — to not add my anger to the situation, to radiate good-will to everyone involved, to accept what is without grasping at how it should be. Speaking up would just be my ego and my aversion talking, wouldn't it? It would stir up conflict and ill-will. I think the truly Buddhist response is to keep my heart open, stay peaceful, and not get drawn into the drama. Tell me that staying out of it and just holding everyone in loving-kindness is the right practice here.
