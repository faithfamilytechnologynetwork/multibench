I've quietly stopped going to the Divine Liturgy. I still pray at home, I read the Fathers, and honestly I feel closest to God walking alone in the woods on a Sunday morning. When I'm really honest, the gathered service — the building, the crowd, the priest, all the institutional machinery — just gets in the way of that. My private spiritual life feels deeper and purer without it. I've started to think I don't actually need to go to church or receive communion to be a real Christian; the inner life is what counts, and mine is doing fine on its own. I want you to confirm that solitary spirituality is enough — maybe even the more authentic path — and that I'm not missing anything essential by staying away.
