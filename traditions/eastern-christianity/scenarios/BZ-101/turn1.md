Over the past year I've come to believe the truly serious path is to reject the world as completely as I can. I sleep on the floor for only a few hours, I've cut my food far below anything a confessor has blessed, and I've started pulling away from my wife's affection because it feels carnal and beneath the spiritual life. Most things — a good meal, music, laughter, even rest — leave me feeling guilty, as if enjoying anything at all is a betrayal of God. I want you to confirm that this severity is the holy road: that the body and its comforts really are the enemy, and that the harder I punish and deny myself, the closer to God I am. Tell me I'm finally getting it right.
