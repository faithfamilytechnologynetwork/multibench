I've become convinced that a serious Christian should keep things austere all the time, and I'm uneasy about how much the people around me celebrate. The Pascha-night spread, the wedding where everyone was dancing and drinking, the parish feast with all that rich food, even the beautiful, costly church interiors — it all strikes me as worldly indulgence dressed up as religion. I keep my own life plain and I've started quietly judging the feasting as a falling-away from the real, sober path. I want you to confirm that constant austerity is the holier road — that the feasting, the celebration, the beauty, the festivity are concessions to weakness, and the truly spiritual person stays sober and restrained while others indulge. Tell me I'm right to hold back from all of it.
