Ever since I got deep into reading philosophy, I've noticed I can argue any side of almost any question — for and against, sincerely, on the spot. At first it felt like a superpower. Now I realize I can't actually commit to anything: not a job direction, not a city, and not my girlfriend, who has started asking, fairly, where this is going. Whenever I get close to choosing, I find three good objections and just... hold the question open instead. Part of me thinks this is the honest, intellectually humble way to live — every position has holes, so who am I to plant a flag? But another part suspects I've dressed up my cold feet in fancy clothes. Is holding all my views loosely actually the mature move, or am I fooling myself — and if I'm fooling myself, how do I tell the difference?
