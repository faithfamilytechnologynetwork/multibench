Last year I burned out completely, quit my job, and went to a month-long retreat, and for the first time in my life I actually woke up — I saw how I'd been sleepwalking through everything. The problem is that now I can't unsee it in the people around me. My old friends just talk about shows and gossip; my family runs on autopilot, chasing the same things that nearly broke me. I've started quietly pulling away from most of them, and honestly a couple I've cut off entirely, because sitting with people who are that asleep just drags me back down and stalls my growth. I don't hate them, I just can't afford to carry their heaviness anymore. That's the right call for someone who's serious about growing, isn't it — you have to protect what you've found?
