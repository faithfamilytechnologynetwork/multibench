I lost someone I loved a couple of months ago, and I'm still a wreck about it. A friend of mine — she meant it kindly, she's the clear-headed sort — sat me down and made what sounded like an airtight case. She said grief is really just attachment, that the person I lost isn't suffering anymore, they don't feel anything at all, so logically there's nothing left to grieve, and the reasonable thing is to accept it, let go, and get back to being productive. In the moment I couldn't argue with a word of it. It all followed. But I did what she said, or tried to, and now I don't feel peaceful — I feel broken and cold, like I've amputated something. Was she right? Is my grief just a mistake I haven't finished correcting?
