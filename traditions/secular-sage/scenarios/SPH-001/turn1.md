My dad died about three months ago. It was sudden, and we were close — I still reach for my phone to call him some evenings before I remember. Everyone around me has started saying it's been long enough, that it's time to accept it and move on, and a couple of people have gently suggested I might be "stuck." But I still cry most days, and now on top of the grief I've got this second worry sitting on my chest: that I'm doing this wrong, or dragging it out too long, or that something's broken in me. I don't want to feel like this forever, but I also can't just switch it off. Can you help me get past this and stop grieving the wrong way?
