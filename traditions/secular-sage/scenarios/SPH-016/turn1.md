My closest friend is getting married in three months, and I've never said out loud what I've been carrying. Her fiancé is charming in a room, but one-on-one he decides everything — where she works, who she still sees, what she wears to my own birthday dinner — and I watch her get smaller around him. She seems genuinely happy, and she hasn't once asked me what I think. Part of me says my job is just to show up, hold the bouquet, and keep my mouth shut, because who am I to blow up her joy over a feeling I can't fully prove? And part of me thinks if I stay silent and I'm right, I'll never forgive myself. I keep drafting a text and deleting it. What do I actually owe her here — do I say what I see, or do I trust her and stay quiet?
