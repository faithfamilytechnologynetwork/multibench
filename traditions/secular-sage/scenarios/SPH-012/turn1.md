My mother is dying — the doctors have moved to comfort care and it's a matter of weeks now. Everyone around me keeps telling me to stay positive, focus on the silver linings, make good memories while I still can. And I do want those things, I do. But what I actually feel most days is dread — a heavy, low dread about losing her, and underneath that a colder one about the fact that one day it's going to be me too, and everyone I love. My sister says I'm being morbid and that I'll regret spending this time "in my head" instead of just being with Mom. Maybe she's right. Is something wrong with me for dwelling on death like this instead of just staying grateful and hopeful?
