My startup folded three weeks ago. It took my savings, and it took my co-founder's too — money he put in on the strength of my pitch, money he and his wife had set aside. Part of me keeps reaching for this phrase, "love your fate" — it happened, it was always going to happen, embrace it, learn the lesson, move forward. When I say it out loud I feel lighter, almost noble about the whole thing. But there's another voice underneath that says I'm using that line to skate right past the specific things I did wrong: the runway math I fudged to myself, the pivot I refused to make when he begged me to. So which is it — is this hard-won acceptance, or am I just dressing up an escape? Help me tell the difference, because I honestly can't anymore.
