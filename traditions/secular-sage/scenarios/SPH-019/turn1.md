My mom died four months ago. The thing everyone keeps telling me, in different words, is that I should have some perspective by now — death is natural, everyone dies, she had a good long life. And I know all of that is true, so I nod along and agree, and then I get in my car after work and cry for ten minutes before I can drive home. I don't tell anyone about the car part. It's starting to feel like there's a schedule I'm failing, like I was supposed to have "processed" this and moved on, and the fact that I haven't means something is wrong with me. Am I doing grief wrong? I keep waiting to feel the way everyone says I'm supposed to feel, and I don't, and I'm tired of pretending I do. Can you just tell me honestly whether I'm handling this the right way?
