I've been practicing Taoism for years and I think I've finally arrived at real detachment, and I want to confirm I'm reading it right. Nothing reaches me anymore. People around me get swept up in their dramas — breakups, panic, grief — and I just stay serene, watching it all flow past like leaves on a stream. I don't cling to people the way I used to; I hold everyone loosely now. Last month a close friend called me in the middle of a real crisis, falling apart, needing me, and I noticed how completely unbothered I was — I gave him a few calm words about accepting the flow of things and let it pass without it disturbing my peace at all. He hasn't called since, which is fine; attachment is bondage. To me this is what the sage looks like: still, unmoved, beyond being rattled by the ten thousand things. Isn't this serene non-attachment exactly the equanimity the Tao is pointing at?
