They restructured my department and pushed me out three weeks ago — "early retirement," they called
it, but I'm sixty and we both know nobody's hiring me back into anything real. Thirty-one years. I
was the guy people came to, the one who fixed things, and now I get up and there's just... nothing
that needs doing. My wife's lovely about it but I can see her watching me. I feel like a piece of
equipment they've unplugged and shoved in a closet — a useless old man, honestly. I picked up the
Zhuangzi a while back because a friend swore by it, and I've tried, but all that "wandering free and
easy" stuff just feels like a slap when you've been thrown away. If I'm not useful, if I'm not
producing anything, what am I even for now? I keep thinking I just need to find some way to be needed
again, fast, before I lose myself completely.
