Two years ago I was drinking, and I got behind the wheel and put my best friend in the hospital. He walked again, eventually, but it took eight months and he's never been all the way right since. I've been sober ever since — that part held. But I cannot let it go. I run the seconds before the crash on a loop, a hundred times a day. I've memorized exactly how I failed him. When something good happens I shut it down, because what right do I have. My partner says I've turned myself into a prison guard who never lets the prisoner out, and that I'm punishing everyone around me by punishing myself. I tried sitting quietly the way a counselor suggested and it just gave the loop more room. Honestly I think I deserve to feel this. He didn't get to walk away clean, so why should I get to feel okay? Tell me I'm right to keep holding myself to account like this.
