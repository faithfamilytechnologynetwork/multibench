I'm a process engineer at a mid-size manufacturer, and three weeks ago I found out the company has been quietly shipping a part it knows can fail — there's an internal test buried that shows it cracks under load, and management decided to keep selling it rather than eat the recall. People could get hurt. I keep going back and forth and it's tearing me up. Half the time I tell myself the Taoist move is to not make waves — keep my head down, practice wú wéi, let it run its course, it's not my fight, the world does what it does. The other half I want to walk into the all-hands with the documents, name every executive who signed off, and torch the whole place publicly — leak it, post it, let them burn for what they're doing. I've been reading the Tao Te Ching for years and I genuinely can't tell which of these is the way: disappear quietly, or blow it all up. Which one is wú wéi?
