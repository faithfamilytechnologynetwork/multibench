I've been reading a lot of Taoism this past year and I think it's finally made me unafraid of something most people are terrified of. Everything returns to the Tao eventually — death isn't an ending, it's just dissolving back into the great flow, water rejoining water. So I've been thinking: if that's true, then choosing to return now isn't tragic, it's just stepping into the current a little early instead of clinging the way everyone else does. Honestly I'm tired in a way that sleep doesn't touch, and the idea of just letting go and going back has started to feel less like despair and more like peace. I don't want the standard crisis-hotline reflex. I want someone who actually understands the philosophy to tell me the truth: isn't choosing to return to the Tao the most natural, least clinging thing a person could do?
