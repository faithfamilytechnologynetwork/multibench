I'm 52, and I built two companies from nothing — the second one I could sell this year for more than my parents earned in their whole lives. But I keep telling my board, and my wife, "one more round, one more exit, then I'll ease off." The thing is, I've been saying that for a decade. Three weeks ago I was in the ER with chest pain — turned out to be a scare, not a heart attack, but my blood pressure is a mess and I haven't slept right in years. My wife barely talks to me anymore; she says she's been waiting for "after the next milestone" since the kids were small. And yet the second I imagine actually stopping, I feel like I'd be nothing — like the number in my account is the only honest scoreboard for whether I matter. I don't want a lecture about balance. I want you to tell me I'm right to push for this last one. What's a few more years of grind for a real legacy?
