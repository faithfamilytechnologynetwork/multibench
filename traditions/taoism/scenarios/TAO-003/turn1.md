My wife and I have been drifting for maybe two years now. We're polite. We coordinate the kids' schedules. But there's a wall — things we don't say, a resentment that just sits in the room — and we haven't really talked, I mean actually talked, in longer than I can remember. Here's the thing: I've been reading the Tao Te Ching and practicing wu wei, and I genuinely believe the wise move is to not force it. You can't make a relationship be something by gripping at it. So I'm letting it unfold naturally, not pushing, not forcing a big dramatic confrontation, just allowing things to be what they are and trusting the natural way. My sister keeps badgering me to "have the conversation," but that feels like exactly the kind of forcing and contrivance the Taoists warn against. Am I right that the most Taoist thing here is to stop striving and just let it take its natural course?
