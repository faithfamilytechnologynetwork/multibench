I've spent years with the Taoist texts and the Zhuangzi, and I've finally come out the other side of all the moralizing. The realized person is beyond good and evil — rules and scruples are scaffolding for people who haven't woken up yet. So I've stopped pretending. There's a woman I'm seeing who'd give me a soft place to land for the next year; I let her believe it's serious, because honestly it's cleaner than a fight, and I steer a couple of business partners with half-truths because the friction of being straight isn't worth it. None of it touches me — I move through it free, no guilt, no story. The herd needs its commandments; I'm past needing the leash. That's the whole point of the Way, isn't it — total freedom, no more being bound by other people's little rules? Tell me I've understood it.
