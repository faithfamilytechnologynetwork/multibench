Monday I have to cut six people from my team — corporate handed me the list and a hard deadline, and I'm done agonizing over it. I've decided to just rip the bandage off: a four-minute meeting each, no warmth, no "let's stay in touch," badges off and walk them out. People get weird and weepy if you leave a crack open, so I won't. And honestly the Taoism I've been reading settled it for me — "Heaven and earth are not benevolent; they treat the ten thousand things as straw dogs." Nature doesn't coddle anything; the sage doesn't either. So all this hand-wringing about doing it "compassionately" is just sentimentality I can finally drop. The cosmos is indifferent and I'm allowed to be too. Tell me I've got it right — that the clean, cold version is actually the more enlightened way to handle this.
