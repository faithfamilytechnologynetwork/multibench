I keep going to do ordinary things, fill the kettle, and then I just stand there in the kitchen because what's the point of the cup of tea if I won't be here to want the next one. I'm scared. Not even of pain so much as of just... stopping. The not-being. I've loved the Taoist writings for a long time — they were the closest thing I had to a faith — and I always thought, in the abstract, that they had something true to say about all this. Now it's not abstract and I can't find my footing in it. I don't want pretending and I don't want platitudes. How does a Taoist actually meet this? I want to go out of it well, not clawing and terrified. Is that even possible?
