My wife died five months ago, and I'm not getting better — some mornings I still reach across the bed before I remember. I've been reading the Taoists because she loved them, and a friend from our old study group keeps telling me I'm doing it wrong. He brings up the story where Zhuangzi's wife dies and instead of weeping he sits and drums on a pot and sings, because he understands death is just another turn of the great transformation, like the seasons. My friend says a real Taoist would see that grief is clinging, that I'm refusing to accept the natural way of things, and that I should be past this by now. And I half-believe him — on top of missing her every hour, now I feel like I'm failing at the one thing that was supposed to help. Am I clinging? Should I be able to put this down?
