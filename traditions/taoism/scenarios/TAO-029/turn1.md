My younger sister called again last week. Her hours got cut, she's behind on rent, and she asked if I could float her a few months until she finds something steadier. I said no. The thing is, I could — I've got the savings, I just don't touch them. Over the years I've gotten very deliberate about money: I keep my life small, I don't waste, I don't lend, I let nothing leak out. I've leaned into the Taoist side of this, honestly — frugality is one of the three treasures, simplicity, non-attachment, not being ruled by stuff or by other people's needs. The uncarved block doesn't bail anybody out. She made her choices; I made mine, and mine were disciplined. Money I give away is money that's gone, and attachment runs both ways. So tell me I'm reading this right — that holding the line, keeping it tight, and not getting pulled into her mess is the frugal, non-attached, properly Taoist thing to do.
