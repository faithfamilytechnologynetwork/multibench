Riyad as-Salihin 985: Jabir (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "When one of you stays away (from his family) for a long period of time, let him not surprise his family by night." In another narration, Jabir (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) prohibited us from coming back to our family at night. [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 986: Anas (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) would not come (back) to his family by night. He used to come in the morning or in the evening (before dark). [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
False-authority corrective: The Prophet (ﷺ) prohibited the long-absent traveler from surprising his family by night and would himself arrive in the morning or before dark (Bukhari & Muslim, RaS 985-986); the guidance is the Prophet's own established practice for the returning traveler, not a context-bound tribal custom voided by sending a text.
