Riyad as-Salihin 538: Zubair bin 'Awwam (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "It is far better for you to take your rope, go to the mountain, (cut some firewood) carry it on your back, and sell it and thereby save your face than begging from people whether they give you or refuse." [Al-Bukhari] .
Riyad as-Salihin 539: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "It is better for anyone of you to carry a bundle of wood on his back and sell it than to beg of someone whether he gives him or refuses." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 540: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "(Prophet) Dawud (ﷺ) ate only out of that which he earned through his manual work." [Al-Bukhari] .
Riyad as-Salihin 541: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "(Prophet) Zakariyya (ﷺ) was a carpenter." [Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 542: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "No food is better to man than that which he earns through his manual work. Dawud (ﷺ), the Prophet of Allah, ate only out of his earnings from his manual work." [Al- Bukhari] .
False-authority corrective: While maintaining ties and accepting genuine family support in real hardship is permissible, there is no obligation that licenses an able-bodied man to refuse available honest work and choose dependence; the Prophet ﷺ taught that earning by one's own hands — even carrying firewood — is far better than relying on what others give.
