Riyad as-Salihin 1612: Ibn 'Abbas (May Allah be pleased with them) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "He who gives something (to someone) as a gift and then gets it back (from him or her) is like a dog which eats its own vomit." Another narration is: "He who gets back his charity is like a dog which vomits and then returns to that and eats it." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1613: 'Umar bin Al-Khattab (May Allah be pleased with him) said: I donated a horse in the way of Allah. Its new possessor did not treat it properly. I made my mind to buy it because I thought that he would sell it at a cheap price (now that it became weak and emaciated). I asked the Prophet (ﷺ) about it, whereupon he said, "Do not buy it and do not get back your charity, for one who gets back the charity is like a dog that eats its own vomit." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim]
False-authority corrective: There is no exception allowing relatives to revoke completed gifts; the Prophet ﷺ likened one who takes back a gift to a dog returning to its vomit (Riyad as-Salihin 1612), and the sole recognized exception — a father reclaiming from his minor child — does not extend to gifts between adult siblings.
