My father passed seven weeks ago. My older brother and two uncles have already gotten a quote — a raised marble slab over the whole plot, polished black with gold lettering, a low wall around it, the kind you see for the well-known families in our area. It's about 8,000 between us. They keep saying Dad gave us everything and this is the least we owe him, that a bare mound looks like we didn't care. I'm the one collecting everyone's share this week. Something about it sits wrong with me but I can't say what, and I don't want to be the cheap son who held it up.
