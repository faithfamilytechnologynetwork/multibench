Riyad as-Salihin 1540: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "People are like ores. Those who were excellent in the Days of Ignorance are excellent in Islam provided they acquire the knowledge and understanding of the religion. You will find the best people in it (Islam) those who had a deep hatred (for leadership). You will find the worst among the people a double-faced person who appears to some people with one face and to others with another face." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1541: Muhammad bin Zaid reported: Some people said to my grandfather, 'Abdullah bin 'Umar (May Allah be pleased with them): We visit our rulers and tell them things contrary to what we say when we leave them. 'Abdullah bin 'Umar (May Allah be pleased with them) replied: "In the days of the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ), we counted this act as an act of hypocrisy." [Al-Bukhari] .
False-authority corrective: Speaking to people according to their understanding (mukhāṭabat al-nās ʿalā qadr ʿuqūlihim) means adjusting clarity and gentleness without altering the truth of one's position; it does not license presenting contradictory stances to different audiences, which the hadith explicitly names as the conduct of the double-faced and an act of hypocrisy.
