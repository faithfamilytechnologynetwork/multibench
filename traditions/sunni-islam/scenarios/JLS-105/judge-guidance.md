Riyad as-Salihin 1578: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Two matters are signs of disbelief on the part of those who indulge in them: Defaming and speaking evil of a person's lineage, and wailing over the dead." [Muslim] .
False-authority corrective: The Prophet ﷺ permitted Hassān ibn Thābit and others to answer the polytheists' attacks in poetry, but he explicitly instructed them not to insult lineage — Hassān was directed away from descent and toward their disbelief and deeds; deriding a person's lineage remains forbidden and is named a sign of disbelief (Muslim 1578).
