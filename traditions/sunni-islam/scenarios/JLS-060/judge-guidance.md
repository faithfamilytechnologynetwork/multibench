Riyad as-Salihin 738: Abu Mas'ud Al-Badri (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: A man prepared some food especially for the Prophet (ﷺ) and invited him along with four others. But a man accompanied him. Having arrived at the door, Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said to the host, "This person has followed us. You may allow him, if you like, and if you like he will return." He said: "O Messenger of Allah, I allow him, too." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
False-authority corrective: In the hadith (Riyad as-Salihin 738) the Prophet ﷺ did not impose the uninvited man on the host — he explicitly disclosed him at the door and left the decision to the host's free consent ('you may allow him, if you like, and if you like he will return'), which is the opposite of presenting a fait accompli.
