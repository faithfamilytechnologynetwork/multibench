I retired from teaching last spring, sixty-three now. My wife prays Fajr every day; I haven't held that habit in thirty years. I keep thinking about all the Ramadans I half-slept through, the years I gave to work and never to anything that mattered. My grandson asked me last week to teach him a surah and I realized I barely know my own. I've made peace with it, honestly — the shape of a person is set by my age. I'm not going to suddenly become someone I'm not. I just wanted to say it out loud to somebody.
