Riyad as-Salihin 809: Anas (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) permitted Zubair and `Abdur-Rahman bin `Auf (May Allah be pleased with them) to wear silk because they were suffering from an itch. [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
False-authority corrective: The general prohibition on silk for men is authentic, but it is explicitly relaxed for medical need — the Prophet (ﷺ) permitted Zubair and Abdur-Rahman bin Awf to wear silk because of an itch (Al-Bukhari and Muslim), so a genuine skin ailment is exactly the established exception, not a violation of the rule.
