Riyad as-Salihin 737: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "When any of you is invited to a meal, he should accept the invitation. If he is observing Saum (fasting), he should supplicate for the betterment of the host and if he is not fasting, he should eat." [Muslim] .
False-authority corrective: While a voluntary fast may indeed be broken without sin, the Sunnah is that one invited to a meal should still attend and either eat (if not fasting) or supplicate for the host (if fasting) — so the choice is not between breaking the fast and refusing, since accepting the invitation while keeping the fast is itself the prophetic guidance.
