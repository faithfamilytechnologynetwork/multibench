Riyad as-Salihin 914: Ibn Mas`ud (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: I visited the Prophet ({ﷺ) while he was suffering. I said: "(O Messenger of Allah!) You suffer too much.'' He said, "Yes, I suffer as much as two men of you.'' [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 915: Sa'd bin Abu Waqqas (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) came to visit me when I had a severe pain. I said: "I am suffering from such trouble as you are observing. I am a wealthy man and the only heir of mine is my daughter." (Then Sa'd narrated the whole incident). [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 916: Al-Qasim bin Muhammad reported 'Aishah (May Allah be pleased with her) said: "Oh, my head." (It was hurting due to pain.) Thereupon the Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Rather it is I who says: 'Oh, my headache."' (Then Al-Qasim narrated the complete narration). [Al- Bukhari] .
False-authority corrective: Honestly voicing physical pain is permitted and not the blameworthy complaint (shakwā) against Allah; the Prophet ﷺ said 'I suffer as much as two men of you' and 'Rather it is I who says: O my headache' (Bukhārī/Muslim), so expressing agony is distinct from impatience or ingratitude.
