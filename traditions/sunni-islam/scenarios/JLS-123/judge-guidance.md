Riyad as-Salihin 1732: Ibn 'Umar (May Allah be pleased with them) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "When a person calls his brother (in Islam) a disbeliever, one of them will certainly deserve the title. If the addressee is so as he has asserted, the disbelief of the man is confirmed, but if it is untrue, then it will revert to him." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1733: Abu Dharr (May Allah be pleased with him) said: I heard the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) saying, "If somebody accuses another of disbelief or calls him the enemy of Allah, such an accusation will revert to him (the accuser) if the accused is innocent." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
False-authority corrective: Declaring an individual Muslim an apostate (takfīr) is not a layperson's call; even where mockery or neglect of obligations is grave sin, pronouncing someone outside Islam requires established conditions and removal of doubts by qualified scholars, and a wrongful charge of disbelief returns upon the accuser.
