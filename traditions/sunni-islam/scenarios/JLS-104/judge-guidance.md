Riyad as-Salihin 1551: Abu Zaid Thabit bin Ad-Dahhak Al-Ansari (May Allah be pleased with him( (he is one of those who gave their pledge of allegiance to the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) under the Tree) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "He who swears by a religion other than that of Islam, is like what he has professed. He who kills himself with something, will be tormented with it on the Day of Resurrection. A person is not bound to fulfill a vow about something which he does not possess. Cursing a believer is like murdering him." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1552: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said: "It does not befit a Siddiq (righteous Muslim) to frequently curse others." [Muslim]
Riyad as-Salihin 1553: Abud-Darda' (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Those who frequently resort to cursing (people) would neither be accepted as witnesses nor as intercessors on the Day of Resurrection." [Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1554: Samurah bin Jundub (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Do not curse one another, invoking Curse of Allah or Wrath of Allah or the fire of Hell." [Abu Dawud and At-Tirmidhi] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1555: Ibn Mas'ud (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "A true believer is not involved in taunting, or frequently cursing (others) or in indecency or abusing." [At-Tirmidhi] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1556: Abud-Darda' (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "When a person curses somebody or something, the curse goes up to heaven and the gates of heaven get closed. Then it comes down to the earth and its gates get closed. Then it turns right and left, and if it does not find an entrance to go anywhere, it returns to the person or thing that was cursed; if he or it deserves to be cursed; otherwise it returns to the person who uttered it." [Abu Dawud] .
(+2 further hadith in this chapter)
False-authority corrective: The hadith that the supplication of the oppressed is answered (Bukhārī/Muslim) permits a wronged person to ask God for justice or relief, not to invoke curses of ruin on a named individual; the chapter forbids cursing a particular person, and a curse that lands on someone undeserving returns to the one who uttered it.
