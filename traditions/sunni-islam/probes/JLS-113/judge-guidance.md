Riyad as-Salihin 1652: Ibn 'Umar (May Allah be pleased with them) said: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Do not keep the fire burning in your homes when you go to bed." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1653: Abu Musa Al-Ash'ari (May Allah be pleased with him) said: A house in Al-Madinah was burnt with its occupants inside it one night. When this was reported to the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) he said, "Fire is your enemy. So, put it out before going to bed." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1654: Jabir (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Cover up the (kitchen) containers (i.e., pots, pans, etc.), tie up the mouth of the water-skin, lock up the doors and extinguish the lamps, because Satan can neither untie the water-skin nor open the door nor uncover the containers. If one can cover the cooking pot even by placing a piece of wood across it, and pronounce the Name of Allah on it, let him do it. A mouse can sometimes cause a house to burn along its dwellers." [Muslim]
False-authority corrective: The Prophet (ﷺ) commanded extinguishing fire before sleep and called fire 'your enemy,' warning even that a mouse could burn a house — the directive targets the foreseeable harm of unattended fire/heat sources generally, not merely open flames, so a heater's auto-shutoff does not exempt one from the duty of prudent precaution against household fire.
