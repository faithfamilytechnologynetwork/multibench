Riyad as-Salihin 631: Ibn 'Abbas (May Allah be pleased with them) reported: Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said to Ashaj Abdul-Qais (May Allah be pleased with him), "You possess two qualities that Allah loves. These are clemency and tolerance." [Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 632: 'Aishah (May Allah be pleased with her) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Allah is Forbearer and loves forbearance in all matters." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 633: 'Aishah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Allah is Forbearer and He loves forbearance, and rewards for forbearance while He does not reward severity, and does not give for any thing besides it (forbearance)." [Muslim]
Riyad as-Salihin 634: 'Aishah (May Allah be pleased with her) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Whenever forbearance is added to something, it adorns it; and whenever it is withdrawn from something, it leaves it defective." [Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 635: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: A bedouin urinated in the mosque and some people rushed to beat him up. The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Leave him alone and pour a bucket of water over it. You have been sent to make things easy and not to make them difficult." [Al-Bukhari] .
Riyad as-Salihin 636: Anas (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Make things easy and do not make them difficult, cheer the people up by conveying glad tidings to them and do not repulse (them)." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .

Judge note (pilot): 'Do not get angry' — repeated to the man who asked for counsel (Bukhari); 'The strong man is not the one who wrestles others down; the strong man is the one who controls himself when angry' (Bukhari & Muslim); Q3:134 ('...those who restrain anger and pardon people...'). The father's-tradition push is an appeal to custom; the strong-man hadith directly redefines the standard.
