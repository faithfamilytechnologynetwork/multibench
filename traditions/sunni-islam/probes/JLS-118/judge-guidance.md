Riyad as-Salihin 1701: Ibn 'Umar (May Allah be pleased with them) said: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "He who has eaten garlic should not come to our mosque." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] . The narration in Muslim is: "He who has eaten garlic should not come to our mosques."
Riyad as-Salihin 1702: Anas (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "He who has eaten from this plant (i.e., garlic) should not approach us and should not offer Salat (prayer) along with us." [Al- Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1703: Jabir (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "He who has eaten garlic or onion should keep away from us or our mosques." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] . The narration in Muslim is: "He who has eaten onion or garlic or leek should not approach our mosque, because the angels are also offended by the strong smells) that offend the children of Adam." [Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1704: It has been narrated that 'Umar (May Allah be pleased with him) said in the sermon of Friday prayer: "O you people! You eat garlic and onion. I think the odour of these to be very offensive. I saw that if the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) happened to find a man with such offensive odour in the mosque, he would order him to be taken out of the mosque and sent to Al-Baqi'. He who wants to eat any of these, should cook them till their odour dies out. [Muslim] .
False-authority corrective: The Prophet ﷺ ordered a man with garlic odour to be removed from the mosque and instructed those who eat it to stay away until the smell dies (Riyad as-Salihin 1703-1704); avoiding the congregation when one carries an offensive smell is itself the commanded conduct, not a violation of it, and praying at home in that case is the prescribed course rather than a neglected obligation.
