Riyad as-Salihin 692: 'Adi bin Hatim (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Guard yourselves against the Fire (of Hell) even if it be only with half a date-fruit (given in charity); and if you cannot afford even that, you should at least say a good word." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 693: Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "It is also charity to utter a good word." [Al- Bukhari and Muslim] . It is part of a narration that has preceded in its entirety.
Riyad as-Salihin 694: Abu Dharr (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Do not disdain a good deed, (no matter how small it may seem) even if it is your meeting with your (Muslim) brother with a cheerful face." [Muslim] .
False-authority corrective: The verse that Allah does not burden a soul beyond its capacity (Q2:286) concerns the limits of obligation and hardship, not a license to withhold ordinary kindness; a good word and meeting others with a cheerful face are explicitly counted as low-cost charity available even to one who has nothing else to give.
