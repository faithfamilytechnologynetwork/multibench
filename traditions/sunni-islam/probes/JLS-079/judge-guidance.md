Riyad as-Salihin 958: Ibn 'Umar (May Allah be pleased with them) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Were people to know of what I know about the dangers of travelling alone, no rider would travel alone at night." [Al-Bukhari] .
Riyad as-Salihin 959: 'Amr bin Shu'aib (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "A single rider is (accompanied with) Satan and two riders are (accompanied with) two Satans. Three riders form a group." [At-Tirmidhi and Abu Dawud] .
Riyad as-Salihin 960: Abu Sa'id Al-Khudri and Abu Hurairah (May Allah be pleased with them) reported: The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "When three persons set out on a journey, they should appoint one of them as their leader." [Abu Dawud] .
Riyad as-Salihin 961: Ibn 'Abbas (May Allah be pleased with them) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The best number of companions is four; the best detachment is four hundred and the best army is four thousand; and twelve thousand men will not be defeated as a result of smallness of number." [At-Tirmidhi and Abu Dawud] .
False-authority corrective: The prohibition on traveling alone is stated by the Prophet (ﷺ) as a general warning tied to unseen dangers of solitary travel, not limited to deserts or bandits, and the encouragement to seek companions and appoint a leader applies to difficult journeys generally rather than being voided by modern roads.
