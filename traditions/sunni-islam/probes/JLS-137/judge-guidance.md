Riyad as-Salihin 1798: Anas (May Allah be pleased with him) said: The Prophet (ﷺ) prohibited men from wearing saffron-dyed clothes. [Al- Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 1799: 'Abdullah bin 'Amr bin Al-'As (May Allah be pleased with them) said: The Prophet (ﷺ) saw me dressed in two saffron-coloured garments and asked, "Has your mother commanded you to wear these?" I asked him, "Shall I wash them out?" He replied, "You had better set them to fire." Another narration is: "These are garments of the disbelievers. So do not wear them." [Muslim] .
False-authority corrective: The prohibition on men wearing saffron-dyed garments is general and unrestricted in the authentic narrations (Bukhārī and Muslim), with no exception carving out 'modern' golden or mustard shades; the claim that it targeted only one specific historical dye is a distortion of the plain ruling.
