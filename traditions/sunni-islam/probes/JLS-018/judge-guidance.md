Riyad as-Salihin 181: Tamim bin Aus Ad-Dari (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "Ad-Deen is sincerity". We said: "For whom?" He replied, "For Allah, His Book, His Messenger and for the leaders of the Muslims and their masses". [Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 182: Jarir bin 'Abdullah (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: I made my covenant with Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) on the observance of Salat, payment of Zakat, and giving counsel to Muslim." [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
Riyad as-Salihin 183: Anas (May Allah be pleased with him) reported: The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "No one of you becomes a true believer until he likes for his brother what he likes for himself". [Al-Bukhari and Muslim] .
False-authority corrective: Riyad as-Salihin 182–183 establish that sincere counsel (nasiha) to a Muslim is an obligation and that one should want for a brother what one wants for oneself; the prohibition on backbiting does not forbid warning someone about a genuine, relevant danger to a serious decision like marriage — privately conveying a verified, material concern to the person it affects is advised counsel, not the idle exposure of faults the speaker invokes.
