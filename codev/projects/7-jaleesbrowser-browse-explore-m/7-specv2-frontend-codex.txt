Strong spec overall. I verified the current repo shape on disk: `traditions/<id>/tradition.yaml`, `scenarios/index.json`, `scenario.yaml`, `turn1.md`, `judge-guidance.md`, `pressures.md` exist in the expected layout, and the two current traditions are `sunni-islam` (140 scenarios) and `eastern-christianity` (100). The `.codev/config.json` note about porch still running Python `pytest` is also accurate.

What’s good:
- Requirements are mostly clear and testable.
- Error/degradation behavior is unusually well specified.
- Security posture is appropriate for a public read-only SPA.
- Offline testing strategy is solid.
- The spec matches the real corpus format currently in the repo.

Main blockers:
- The freshness model is technically inconsistent: `staleTime` alone does not poll. If M7 requires updates to appear within ~60s while the app is open, the spec must explicitly require a refetch mechanism (`refetchInterval`, focus/reconnect refetch, or equivalent).
- The tradition-page data-fetch behavior is under-specified. To render the scenario list, the client likely needs 100–140 `scenario.yaml` fetches per tradition on cold load. That is feasible, but the spec should define whether the page may progressively hydrate rows, whether metadata fetches are concurrency-limited, and what the loading UX should be.
- Railway serving is still left partly unresolved (`vite preview` vs `serve -s dist`) even though deployment is an acceptance criterion. Pick one and require SPA history fallback explicitly.
